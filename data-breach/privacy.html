Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 6, 2026

Entegrity is a secret-sharing game hosted at entegrity.gg. The short version: we collect almost nothing, because there's almost nothing to collect.

1. What Entegrity does

You upload a photo or write a message. It gets encrypted in your browser and turned into a shareable link. The recipient plays a 5-level game to unlock it. That's the whole product.

2. What we collect

Nothing on a server. Entegrity has no accounts, no logins, no analytics SDKs, and no database storing your secrets.

  • Your secret: Encrypted entirely in your browser using AES-256-GCM with a key derived via PBKDF2 (100,000 iterations). The encrypted payload lives inside the URL itself. We never receive it and cannot read it.
  • The decryption key: Split into 5 fragments, one revealed per game level. Generated client-side. Never transmitted.
  • Cookies / local storage: No tracking cookies. The game may use localStorage to remember in-progress game state on your own device.

3. Hosting metadata

Like any website, our static hosting provider automatically processes standard HTTP request metadata (IP, user agent, referrer, timestamp) to deliver the page. We do not aggregate, store, or analyze this ourselves.

4. Links to Ente

Entegrity is built by the team behind Ente. Following links to ente.com is governed by Ente's own privacy policy.

5. Third parties

We do not share data with third parties because we do not have data to share. There are no ad networks, analytics vendors, or third-party trackers embedded in Entegrity.

6. Children

Entegrity is a cartoon platformer suitable for general audiences. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children.

7. Your rights

Because we do not store personal data, there is nothing for us to access, export, or delete on your behalf. To "delete" a secret you've shared, simply stop sharing the link — the encrypted payload only exists wherever that link exists.

8. Security

All encryption uses the browser's Web Crypto API. The source code is open and auditable on GitHub. No cryptographic system is perfect; share sensitive secrets at your own discretion.

9. Changes

We may update this policy. Material changes will be reflected by updating the date above.

10. Contact

Questions? Open an issue on the GitHub repo.
